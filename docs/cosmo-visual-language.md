# Cosmo Visual Language System

> **Single Source of Truth** for all future imagery, media direction, gallery systems, before/after visuals, homepage consistency, and AI image generation.

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Active  
**Scope:** All Cosmo Skin Clinic visual media — hero imagery, treatment visuals, gallery assets, before/after systems, editorial content.

---

## Section 1 — Brand Visual Philosophy

### Emotional Identity

Cosmo Skin Clinic communicates through **luxury through restraint**. Every visual decision should prioritize calm sophistication over flashy impact. The brand occupies a refined space between medical credibility and editorial elegance.

**The site should feel:**
- **Cinematic** — images evoke atmosphere and mood, not just clinical documentation
- **Premium** — every pixel communicates care and deliberate curation
- **Calm** — visual hierarchy breathes; nothing shouts
- **Editorial** — composition, lighting, and framing rival luxury magazine spreads
- **Luxurious through restraint** — quality over quantity; negative space is a feature
- **Medically sophisticated** — competence, precision, and trust are visually implicit

**The site should NOT feel:**
- Flashy or attention-seeking
- Hyper-clinical (sterile white, cold blue)
- Influencer beauty brand
- Startup-tech aesthetic
- Cyberpunk or futuristic
- Glossy AI marketing
- Overanimated template

### Core Principle

> *"Could this image naturally belong on the homepage?"*

If a piece of media would feel out of place beside the cinematic hero and editorial typography of the homepage, it should not enter the ecosystem.

## Section 2 — Color Language

### Approved Palette

Cosmo's color palette is deliberately restrained — deep, warm, and atmospheric. The goal is tonal cohesion, not variety.

| Token | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| `--color-bg` | `#0d0d1a` | Primary background (deep navy) |
| `--color-bg-secondary` | `#1a1a2e` | Alternate sections, dropdown menus |
| `--color-bg-card` | `#16213e` | Card backgrounds, form surfaces |
| `--color-gold` | `#c9a96e` | Primary accent — CTAs, highlights, brand marks |
| `--color-gold-light` | `#e8c98a` | Hover states, brighter accent moments |
| `--color-gold-dark` | `#a07840` | Subtle depth, decorative gradients |
| `--color-white` | `#f9f6f1` | Primary headings, key text (warm off-white) |
| `--color-white-muted` | `#d4cfc8` | Secondary text, metadata |
| `--color-text` | `#e8e4de` | Body text (warm light gray) |
| `--color-text-muted` | `#9e9a94` | Supporting text, captions |
| `--color-border` | `rgba(201, 169, 110, 0.2)` | Subtle dividers, card borders |
| `--color-border-strong` | `rgba(201, 169, 110, 0.5)` | Emphasis borders, hover states |

### Gold Philosophy

Gold in the Cosmo system is **warm and muted** — never neon, never orange-heavy, never brassy.
- Gold should feel like aged precious metal, not synthetic highlight
- Gold accents are used sparingly to guide attention, not decorate
- The gold tones sit at approximately 60–70% saturation to maintain warmth without vulgarity

### Where Each Tone Is Used

- **Deep Navy (`#0d0d1a`)** creates the immersive atmospheric foundation — it is the canvas upon which all content sits
- **Secondary Navy (`#1a1a2e`)** provides subtle section alternation without breaking the dark immersion
- **Gold** is reserved for interactive elements, brand identity, and directional cues — it is the only saturated accent

## Section 3 — Lighting Philosophy

### Core Direction

Lighting is the primary tool for creating mood and depth. Every generated or selected image should exhibit deliberate lighting direction.

**Approved lighting characteristics:**
- **Directional lighting** — clear light source with intentional shadow casting
- **Warm edge lighting** — soft golden rim light that creates separation and depth
- **Cinematic contrast** — controlled dynamic range with deep shadows and controlled highlights
- **Controlled shadows** — shadows that add dimension without crushing detail
- **Soft reflections** — subtle bounce light that reveals form without glare

**Avoid:**
- Flat, even lighting with no directional source
- Blown-out highlights or clipped whites
- Influencer beauty lighting (overhead ring light, flat front-facing fill)
- Overexposed white clinic interiors
- Light that competes with or overpowers the subject

### Environmental Context

In treatment room imagery, the primary light source should feel naturalistic — warm ambient light supplemented by equipment glow. This creates the premium atmosphere where clinical precision meets luxury comfort.

## Section 4 — Composition Rules

### Framing Philosophy

Centered, symmetrical stock-photo compositions are discouraged. Cosmo imagery favors editorial, asymmetrical framing that feels candid and curated.

**Approved composition principles:**
- **Asymmetrical framing** — subjects placed off-center to create visual tension and interest
- **Environmental storytelling** — the space around the subject conveys context and atmosphere
- **Negative space usage** — generous breathing room creates luxury calm; text overlays sit in negative space
- **Layered depth** — foreground, subject, and background create dimensional immersion
- **Restrained focus hierarchy** — one clear focal point per image; no competing elements

### Aspect Ratio Considerations

- Wider compositions (16:9, 3:2) suit hero and editorial use
- Square or near-square (1:1, 4:3) suit cards and grids
- Vertical compositions (3:4, 9:16) suit mobile-first layouts
- Extreme crops (panoramic, ultra-wide) are discouraged

### Why Not Centered

Centered compositions read as stock photography. They lack the editorial intent and candid sophistication that premium brands communicate. Asymmetry signals curation, intentionality, and confidence.

## Section 5 — Treatment Visual Philosophy

### Visual Direction by Treatment

All treatment imagery should convey **luxury procedural calm** — never aggressive cosmetic surgery marketing.

| Treatment | Visual Direction | Mood |
|-----------|-----------------|------|
| Microneedling | Close clinical framing with warm ambient light; focus on practitioner's hands and precision tool | Controlled, precise, serene |
| HydraFacial | Glowing skin with soft diffusion; device interaction visible but not dominant | Renewing, fresh, luminous |

### Unified Approach

Despite different treatments, all imagery should feel visually consistent — same lighting philosophy, same color temperature, same compositional restraint.

## Section 6 — Human Presence Rules

### When People Appear

Human presence should feel contextual, not performative. The patient is never the primary spectacle; the treatment, atmosphere, or result is.

**Allowed:**
- **Silhouettes** — patient as compositional element rather than focal point
- **Partial treatment interaction** — hands, profiles, or backs during procedure
- **Restrained procedural context** — practitioner's hands in frame, patient visible only partially
- **Environmental scale** — person used to establish spatial context of the treatment room

**Avoid:**
- Exaggerated expressions of joy, relief, or surprise
- Influencer posing — chin-forward, hand-on-cheek, mirror selfie angles
- Uncanny AI beauty faces — hyper-symmetrical, poreless, plastic-skin renderings
- Fake perfection skin — overly smooth, no texture, no natural imperfection
- Direct eye contact with camera in treatment context

### The Doctor

When Dr. Alexandra Rivera appears in imagery, the tone should be: **approachable expertise** — clinical authority softened by warm, attentive presence. Not distant, not overly casual.

## Section 7 — Image Ratio Standards

### Approved Aspect Ratios

| Context | Aspect Ratio | Notes |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| Homepage hero | 16:9 (landscape) or wider | Full-width cinematic; image must contain left-side negative space for text overlay |
| Treatment hero | 16:9 (landscape) | Consistent with homepage hero treatment |
| Editorial content | 3:2 (landscape) or 4:3 | Versatile for section headers, split layouts |
| Treatment cards | 16:10 (landscape) | Consistent with `.treatment-card-image` aspect ratio |
| Before/after slider | 4:3 (landscape) | Side-by-side comparison format |
| Gallery grid | 4:3 or 3:2 (landscape) | Unified gallery presentation |
| Doctor/people | 3:4 (portrait) | Professional portraiture ratio |
| Social media | 1:1 (square) or 4:5 (portrait) | Secondary — for social distribution only |
| Thumbnails | 16:9 (landscape) | Blog cards, condition cards |

### Mobile Crop Considerations

- **Hero images:** Maintain focal point visibility when cropped to narrower viewports. The treatment chair and equipment must remain visible on mobile.
- **Treatment imagery:** Center on the treatment interaction area. Avoid crops that isolate equipment without context.
- **Before/after:** Use vertical orientation or stacked layout on mobile; never crop the comparison area.
- **General rule:** Design for 375–430px width as the mobile baseline. Verify focal preservation at this scale.

## Section 8 — Overlay & Atmosphere Rules

### Overlay Standards

When text sits over imagery, overlays must be subtle enough to preserve cinematic quality while ensuring readability.

**Approved techniques:**
- **Subtle readability gradients** — linear gradients that darken only the area behind text, never the full image
- **Atmospheric blending** — bottom-edge fades that dissolve the image into the page background (see `.hero-home::after` implementation)
- **Cinematic preservation** — overlays should use `rgba(13, 13, 26, 0.82)` at maximum; higher opacity breaks the cinematic feel
- **Negative-space respect** — overlays must not cover visual anchor points (treatment chair, equipment, lighting elements)

### Homepage Hero Overlay (Reference Implementation)

```css
background: linear-gradient(
  to right,
  rgba(13, 13, 26, 0.82) 0%,    /* readability at left edge */
  rgba(13, 13, 26, 0.25) 40%,   /* gentle transition */
  transparent 62%                /* equipment fully visible on right */
);
```

### Mobile Overlay Shift

On narrow viewports (<1200px), the overlay shifts from left-to-right to top-to-bottom to accommodate stacked content:

```css
background: linear-gradient(
  to bottom,
  rgba(13, 13, 26, 0.7) 0%,     /* text area readability */
  rgba(13, 13, 26, 0.3) 50%,    /* gentle transition */
  transparent 80%                /* equipment visible at bottom */
);
```

### What to Avoid

- Muddy overlays that darken the entire image uniformly
- Crushed shadows — loss of detail in dark areas
- Excessive darkening — opacity above 0.85 breaks image depth
- Heavy blur — backdrop-filter values above 16px feel artificial
- Visible gradient bands — use sufficient gradient stops to ensure smooth transitions

## Section 9 — Animation Philosophy

### Allowed

Cosmo animations are **restrained and calm** — they should serve the user experience, not showcase animation capability.

- **Subtle fades** — opacity transitions on scroll entry (`.animate-on-scroll`)
- **Restrained hover lifts** — `transform: translateY(-4px)` with shadow enhancement
- **Calm transitions** — standard duration 0.25s–0.3s using cubic bezier easing (`cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.46, 0.45, 0.94)`)
- **Gold underline reveal** — hover underline animation on navigation links (width transition from 0→100%)

### Avoid

- **Parallax spam** — multi-layer scrolling effects that compete with content
- **Floating particles** — decorative particle systems add no value and feel generic
- **Flashy zoom effects** — aggressive scale transforms on entry
- **Aggressive motion systems** — complex keyframe sequences, bouncing elements, rotating decorations
- **Loading animations** — the site is static; no loading spinners, skeletons, or preloader animations
- **Infinite scroll animations** — elements should animate once on entry, not loop

### Performance Rule

All animations must use GPU-accelerated properties only: `transform`, `opacity`, `filter`. Never animate `width`, `height`, `top`, `left`, `margin`, or `padding`.

## Section 10 — Mobile Media Rules

### Focal Preservation

On mobile, the most important visual anchor must remain visible and recognizable. Secondary areas may be cropped.

**Hierarchy of preservation (hero context):**
1. Treatment chair (primary visual anchor)
2. Monitor/equipment (contextual anchor)
3. Lighting ring and warm vertical lighting strip (atmospheric anchor)
4. General ambient environment (secondary)

### Responsive Crop Philosophy

- **Hero background:** Use `object-position` or `background-position` to ensure key anchors remain in frame
- **Treatment imagery:** Center crop on the treatment interaction; equipment may be partially cropped
- **Portrait/card imagery:** Face or subject must remain visible; background may be cropped
- **Never use CSS `background-size: contain`** on hero or full-width imagery — always `cover`

### Spacing Rhythm on Mobile

- Overlays should not feel dense on small screens
- Text blocks should have generous padding (minimum 1rem from edges on 375px viewport)
- CTA buttons stack vertically below 600px viewport width
- Trust indicators and metadata wrap naturally; dividers hide below 600px

### Avoiding Image Chaos

- One clear focal point per image — no busy compositions
- When multiple elements compete, simplify the crop
- Text must never sit over busy image areas on mobile
- If an image does not have clear negative space, it requires a stronger overlay on mobile

## Section 11 — Strict "Do NOT" List

These are **non-negotiable** visual identity guardrails. Any image that violates these rules must be rejected or regenerated.

| ❌ Forbidden | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| **Neon gold** (#ffd700, #ffcc00, or any high-saturation yellow-gold) | Destroys the muted, aged-precious-metal feel of the brand |
| **Glossy AI skin** — poreless, plastic, hyper-smooth renderings | Reads as fake; destroys medical credibility and trust |
| **White corporate clinics** — bright white walls, fluorescent lighting, sterile hallways | Contradicts the deep-navy luxury atmosphere entirely |
| **Random stock imagery** — generic smiling models, unrelated medical stock photos | Destroys visual cohesion; immediately reads as template |
| **Hyper-saturated beauty ads** — high-contrast skin, glowing highlighter, fashion-editorial lighting | Reads as beauty influencer, not medical premium |
| **Futuristic hologram aesthetics** — glowing UI overlays, AR interfaces, holographic displays | Contradicts the restrained editorial tone |
| **Overcrowded layouts** — multiple focal points, busy backgrounds, layered graphics | Destroys calm hierarchy; feels chaotic and cheap |
| **Visual inconsistency between pages** — different lighting, different color temperature, different aesthetic on different pages | The cardinal sin; every page must feel like it belongs to the same brand |
| **Centered stock-photo composition** — subject dead-center with equal headroom | Reads as template, not editorial |
| **Exaggerated patient expressions** — open-mouthed joy, surprised eyebrows, dramatic reactions | Feels performative and inauthentic |
| **Parallax scrolling, floating particles, or animated backgrounds** | Overdesigned; competes with content |

## Section 12 — Final Consistency Rule

### The Homepage Test

> **"Could this image naturally belong on the homepage?"**

Before any image enters the Cosmo ecosystem, ask:

1. Does this image share the same **lighting temperature** as the homepage hero?
2. Does this image share the same **color palette** (deep navy, warm gold, muted ivory)?
3. Does this image share the same **compositional restraint**?
4. Would this image feel **visually cohesive** if placed beside the current homepage hero?
5. Does this image communicate **luxury through restraint**?

If the answer to any of these is **no**, the image does not belong in the Cosmo visual ecosystem.

### Ecosystem Integrity

The homepage hero (`assets/images/home-page.png`) is the **visual north star**. All future images — treatment visuals, gallery assets, before/after comparisons, editorial content — must feel visually consistent with this reference image.

When generating or selecting new media, open the homepage side-by-side with the candidate image. If they do not feel like they belong to the same brand, the candidate must be adjusted or rejected.
