Understanding Your Skin

What Are Stretch Marks?

Stretch marks develop when the skin stretches rapidly, causing collagen and elastin to rupture. Our treatments help rebuild this damaged tissue.

Common Signs

  • Visible indented lines or striae
  • Changes in skin texture
  • Color variation from pink to silver
  • Textural difference from surrounding skin

Root Causes

  • Pregnancy and postpartum changes
  • Rapid weight gain or loss
  • Growth spurts during puberty
  • Hormonal fluctuations

Recovery & Aftercare

Supporting your skin's healing process is essential for optimal results.

Keep Area Moisturised

Apply nourishing creams to treated areas. Well-hydrated skin responds better to treatment.

Sun Protection

Protect treated areas from UV exposure. New collagen is particularly susceptible to damage.

Maintain Results

Stay hydrated, maintain stable weight, and follow your skincare routine for lasting improvements.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Can stretch marks be completely removed? +

While complete removal isn't always possible, significant reduction is achievable. Most patients see 50-70% improvement with a course of treatments.

How many treatments will I need? +

Most patients require 4-8 sessions, spaced 4-6 weeks apart. Older, established stretch marks typically need more treatment than newer ones.

When will I see results? +

Results develop gradually over 3-6 months as collagen remodels. Initial improvements may be visible within 4-6 weeks.
