Understanding Your Skin

Beyond Discolouration

Pigmentation irregularities manifest in many forms—sun spots, melasma, post-inflammatory marks—and each has distinct triggers and treatment considerations. Understanding which type you have is the first step toward effective correction. Some pigmentation is surface-level and responds quickly; deeper discolouration requires more measured, layered care.

Our approach combines advanced treatment technologies with medical-grade skincare to not only reduce existing pigmentation but also address underlying causes and prevent new spots from forming. We recognise that darker skin tones require particular care, and our protocols reflect this expertise. Whether your concern is sun-related, hormonal, or post-inflammatory, the path to clarity begins with an accurate understanding of your skin’s unique behaviour.

Common Signs

  • Dark spots and patches
  • Uneven skin tone and texture
  • Sun damage and age spots
  • Melasma and chloasma

Root Causes

  • Cumulative UV exposure damage
  • Hormonal fluctuations and pregnancy
  • Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation
  • Natural melanin overproduction

Who This Helps Most

  • Uneven skin tone and patchy discolouration
  • Post-acne marks and lingering dark spots
  • Dullness and tired-looking complexion
  • Mild to moderate sun-related pigmentation
  • Texture concerns overlapping with pigmentation
  • Melasma patients seeking gentle correction

After Your Treatment

Recovery & Aftercare

Proper aftercare is the most important part of achieving and maintaining your results.

Strict Sun Protection

UV exposure is the single most common trigger for pigmentation recurrence. Apply SPF 50+ broad-spectrum protection every morning, rain or shine, and reapply every two hours during direct sun exposure. This is non-negotiable for protecting your results.

Gentle Recovery Period

Depending on treatment depth, you may experience mild redness, temporary sensitivity, or light flaking for a few days. This is normal and part of the skin’s renewal process. Stick to a gentle cleanser and moisturiser during this window.

Expected: Mild redness or warmth for 24–48 hours. Light flaking around day 3–5 as skin renews.

Avoid: Active acids, retinols, and harsh exfoliants for at least 5–7 days post-treatment.

Hydration & Nourishment

Incorporate gentle, pigment-supporting ingredients like vitamin C, niacinamide, and licorice extract into your routine as your skin tolerates them. These help suppress melanin production and enhance treatment results over time. Drink plenty of water and prioritise barrier-supporting moisturisers.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Will pigmentation treatments work on dark skin tones? +

Absolutely, and with careful expertise. Darker skin contains more melanin, making it more prone to pigmentation but also requiring adjusted treatment parameters to avoid causing further discoloration. Our practitioners have extensive experience treating all skin tones and select appropriate peel depths, energy settings, and aftercare protocols accordingly.

What's the difference between sun spots and melasma? +

Sun spots are discrete, flat areas of increased pigmentation caused by UV exposure, typically appearing on frequently exposed areas. Melasma, however, is a chronic condition featuring larger, irregular patches often triggered by hormonal changes—pregnancy, oral contraceptives, or hormone therapy. Melasma is more challenging to treat and requires a gentler, more comprehensive approach.

How many treatments are needed for pigmentation? +

This varies significantly based on pigmentation type, depth, and skin response. Superficial sun spots may respond to 1-3 treatments, while deeper melasma often requires 6-8 sessions with maintenance. We'll assess your specific concerns during consultation and outline a realistic treatment timeline with expected outcomes.

Can pigmentation come back after treatment? +

Pigmentation can recur, especially with continued sun exposure or hormonal triggers. However, our treatments don't just fade existing spots—they improve the skin's overall health and resilience. Maintenance skincare and periodic treatments keep pigmentation under control. We'll equip you with knowledge and products to protect your results long-term.
