Understanding Your Skin

Beyond Discolouration

Pigmentation irregularities manifest in many forms—sun spots, melasma, post-inflammatory marks—and each has distinct triggers and treatment considerations. Understanding which type you have is the first step toward effective correction.

Our approach combines advanced treatment technologies with medical-grade skincare to not only reduce existing pigmentation but also address underlying causes and prevent new spots from forming. We recognise that darker skin tones require particular care, and our protocols reflect this expertise.

Common Signs

  • Dark spots and patches
  • Uneven skin tone and texture
  • Sun damage and age spots
  • Melasma and chloasma

Root Causes

  • Cumulative UV exposure damage
  • Hormonal fluctuations and pregnancy
  • Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation
  • Natural melanin overproduction

After Your Treatment

Recovery & Aftercare

Proper aftercare is the most important part of achieving and maintaining your results.

Strict Sun Protection

Pigmentation worsens with UV exposure. Apply SPF 50+ broad-spectrum protection every morning, rain or shine, and reapply every 2 hours during direct sun exposure.

Pigmentation-Fighting Skincare

Incorporate tyrosinase inhibitors like vitamin C, niacinamide, and licorice extract into your routine. These ingredients suppress melanin production and enhance treatment results.

Patience and Consistency

Pigmentation treatment is gradual. Expect 8-12 weeks to see significant improvements, with continued refinement over months. Consistency with both treatments and skincare is essential.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Will pigmentation treatments work on dark skin tones? +

Absolutely, and with careful expertise. Darker skin contains more melanin, making it more prone to pigmentation but also requiring adjusted treatment parameters to avoid causing further discoloration. Our practitioners have extensive experience treating all skin tones and select appropriate peel depths, energy settings, and aftercare protocols accordingly.

What's the difference between sun spots and melasma? +

Sun spots are discrete, flat areas of increased pigmentation caused by UV exposure, typically appearing on frequently exposed areas. Melasma, however, is a chronic condition featuring larger, irregular patches often triggered by hormonal changes—pregnancy, oral contraceptives, or hormone therapy. Melasma is more challenging to treat and requires a gentler, more comprehensive approach.

How many treatments are needed for pigmentation? +

This varies significantly based on pigmentation type, depth, and skin response. Superficial sun spots may respond to 1-3 treatments, while deeper melasma often requires 6-8 sessions with maintenance. We'll assess your specific concerns during consultation and outline a realistic treatment timeline with expected outcomes.

Can pigmentation come back after treatment? +

Pigmentation can recur, especially with continued sun exposure or hormonal triggers. However, our treatments don't just fade existing spots—they improve the skin's overall health and resilience. Maintenance skincare and periodic treatments keep pigmentation under control. We'll equip you with knowledge and products to protect your results long-term.
